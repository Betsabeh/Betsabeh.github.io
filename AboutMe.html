About Me

I am Betsabeh Tanoori, and I completed my PhD in Computer Engineering, specializing in Artificial Intelligence, at Shiraz University in 2021. My doctoral research focused on semi-supervised approaches for drug-target interaction identification, conducted under the supervision of Mansoor Zolghadri Jahromi and the guidance of Dr. Eghbal G Mansoori and Dr.Mohammad Taheri.

In my work, I applied both classification and regression models to predict drug targets, leveraging the advantages of fusing multiple biological data sources, transfer learning, and semi-supervised learning techniques.

Additionally, I have experience in brain volumetry of MRI images, collaborating with Professor.Zohreh Azimifar and Professor.Serajdean katebi. This research involved classifying brain images using Support Vector Machines (SVM) and calculating brain volume.

Currently, I am serving as an Assistant Professor at the Zand Institute of Higher Education, where I continue to engage in research and teaching, aiming to inspire the next generation of engineers and researchers.
